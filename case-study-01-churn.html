Overview

Why are customers leaving?

A telecom operator was experiencing a 27.3% annual churn rate — nearly double the 14% industry benchmark. Revenue leakage was significant, but the business had no visibility into which segments were at risk or what was driving exits.

The dataset: 7,043 customer records across 21 attributes spanning contract type, tenure, service bundling, monthly charges, and payment method. The challenge was transforming this raw data into a reliable segmentation and retention framework.

"Month-to-month customers churned at 42.7% — fifteen times higher than two-year contract holders."

Key Findings

Four signals the data couldn't hide

Each finding directly informed a retention strategy recommendation in the final dashboards.

Finding · 01
15×
Contract Type is the #1 Churn Driver
Month-to-month customers churn 15× more than two-year subscribers. This single variable explains 38% of total churn variance in the dataset.
Finding · 02
35%
New Customers Are Most Vulnerable
Customers in their first 12 months churn at 35%. The critical intervention window is months 2–6, where support touchpoints have the highest ROI.
Finding · 03
2×
Paperless Billing Correlates with Higher Churn
33.6% of paperless billing users churned vs 16.3% of traditional billing customers — a digital-native segment with lower loyalty signals.
Finding · 04
↓18pp
Service Bundling Strongly Reduces Churn
Adding Tech Support alone drops churn by 18 percentage points. Customers with 3+ services churn at only 8.9% — the lowest of any segment.
