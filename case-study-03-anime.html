Overview

What makes
anime tick?

The global anime market exceeded $25 billion in 2023 — yet surprisingly little structured analysis existed on what actually drives a title's success. Is it the genre? The studio? The episode count? The season it airs?

This project used a 530-title Kaggle dataset enriched with MyAnimeList (MAL) rating data to treat anime as a genuine business intelligence challenge — applying the same rigour as a product analytics study to an entertainment domain.

"Action titles showed the widest ratings variance — beloved peaks and catastrophic flops, rarely anything in between."

Key Insights

What the data revealed

Each insight was derived from statistical analysis — not assumption — and translated directly into recommendations for the final Power BI visuals.

Insight · 01
8.5/10
Sci-Fi Leads All Genres in Average Rating
Driven by a handful of critically acclaimed franchises, Sci-Fi pulls the genre mean up significantly. Fantasy follows closely at 8.2/10.
Insight · 02
σ 1.82
Action Has the Widest Rating Variance
Action produces the most beloved titles and the worst-rated ones — the genre is polarising by nature. High-risk, high-reward for studios.
Insight · 03
−0.41
Episode Count Hurts Quality Past 50
Beyond 50 episodes, a moderate negative correlation (r = −0.41) emerges between episode count and rating. Optimal quality clusters at 12–24 episodes.
Insight · 04
Spring
Spring Releases Outperform Other Seasons
Spring-season premieres score 0.3 rating points higher on average — likely due to higher-budget productions targeting the April broadcast window.
Custom Metrics

Three metrics engineered from scratch

None of these existed in the raw dataset. Each captures a dimension of performance invisible to standard rating analysis alone.

📡
Engagement Score
rating × log(member_count)
Combines critical quality with audience reach. A title rated 9.0 with 500K members scores higher than a 9.5 with 20K — popularity matters as much as quality.
📊
Genre Saturation Index
genre_count / total_titles
Measures how crowded a genre is relative to the full dataset. High saturation (Action, Comedy) means more competition; low saturation (Horror, Sports) means opportunity.
🏆
Studio Consistency Rating
1 − (std_dev / mean_rating)
Measures how reliably a studio delivers quality. Madhouse and Bones score high (consistent quality floor); Toei scores low (high variance across titles).
